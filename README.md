# Funkys-Hot-Sauce-Factory-Customer-Analysis

Customer Insights and Consumption Behavior Analysis

Background:
Understanding customer spending behavior is essential for driving business growth. 
In this project, I analyzed online customer spending behavior at Funky’s Hot Sauce Factory, a hot sauce brand headquartered in Bellingham, WA, with a customer base spanning North America.
My goals were to uncover what factors drive their online sales revenue, identify high-value spending tiers, and pinpoint growth opportunities.
Using SQL, R, and Google Sheets, I transformed raw Shopify data into actionable insights around customer segmentation, marketing effectiveness, and geographic performance.

Goals:
* Identify patterns in customer spending and purchasing behavior
* Evaluate the relationship between marketing subscription status (email/SMS opt-in rates) and total spending
* Analyze geographic distribution of online sales revenue across regions
* Provide data-driven recommendations to support business growth and customer engagement

Tools/Methodology:
* Shopify CSV data (source dataset)
* MySQL (data cleaning, transformation, and aggregation)
* R (statistical analysis, hypothesis testing, and data visualization)
* Google Sheets (data visualization)
* Data preprocessing and feature selection
* Descriptive statistics and distribution analysis
* Shapiro-Wilk test for normality
* Wilcoxon rank-sum test (non-parametric hypothesis testing)
* Customer segmentation
* Geographic analysis

Key Findings:
* Strong revenue concentration among top customers
  * The top 20% of customers generated about 46% of total revenue, while the top 60% contributed over 80%.
* Marketing subscribers spend a statistically significant amount more thank non-subscribers
  * Customers subscribed to email/SMS marketing spent approximately 39% more on average, with statistical significance confirmed.
* Large proportion of non-spending customers
  * About 41% of registered online customers have yet to make a purchase, representing opportunities for conversion.
* Revenue is geographically concentrated
  * About 46.6% of total revenue comes from Washington, U.S., with top-performing cities clustered near the company’s headquarters.
* Spending distribution is non-normal and right-skewed

Recommendations:
* Focus on increasing marketing acceptance through targeted incentives.
* Convert non-spending customers by developing strategies such as first-purchase discounts, personalized outreach, and campaigns
* Retain and leverage high-value customers through loyalty programs, bundles, and/or repeat-purchase incentives
* Targeted marketing in underperforming regions to diversify the customer base.
* Leverage social media and brand exposure by partnering with media presences.
